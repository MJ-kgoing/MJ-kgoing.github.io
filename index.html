Iget reflection|02

Posted on 2020-03-15

Iget (得到) - an Chinese app that contains original audio courses on different subjects, teaching by expers and professionists.

Course taking: The psychology of self-developing. (自我发展心理学)

About This Talk ( 14 | The “Should Be” Thinking Mode Toward Yourself: Why Can’t You Accept Your Emotions? )

  1. “Should be” thinking mode

    The “should be” thinking mode is a way of thinking that has the essence of rejecting the real world, which we try to make it surrender to the existing rules in our mind, and expressing emotions like anxiety, anger, or depress when the world doesn’t obey to those rules. There are two types of “should be” thinking mode: one is for the world and one is for ourselves. This talk mainly focuses on the latter.

  1. Examples given for explanation.

    The author has a friend who just realized it was the time to be hardworking, therefore he sleeps 6 hours only per day even during weekend and tries his best to learn something new. However, altough he feels awesome while studying, every time his productivity decreases, what he feels instead is dispirition. This gives him an ambivalent emotion that he feels working so hard is not worthy but he would be a looser if he gives up as there are people working even harder.

    The author pointed out that the cause of this contradicting emotion is the “should be” thinking of “I should be hard working”. Naturally, striving occurs when people have a goal and they work actively toward it. However, hard-working without a goal would just be the copy version of the real one as we only know it is the right thing to do instead of what we really want to succeed. It seems that we are staying up late, listening to lectures and doing extra work, but what these compose is only the status of “working hard”.

Personal Reflection

There has been a period of time that I feel overwelmed and extremely fatigued, and, anxiety. Everyday, I felt that there were countless tasks to be finished as you can always do extra. For example, if I finished today’s homework, I would think about completing tomorrow’s and revise and review and… I believed I was hardworking as I was like a spinning top everyday. However, on the other hand I feel no accomplishment or improve but just fatigue and anxiety. I liked the status of working hard as it made me feel I was going forward, but I was also doubting myself and the meaning of all of these. I was really puzzled. After listening to this talk, I think I found the answer. I should have a clearer goal, which would tell me what should I do. In addition, I should find the way of study which is suitable for me. Nobody says you have to stay up if you want to success. Listening to your heart and stop mimicing those “you should” statements the world is instilling to you. Let yourself pause for a while, take a breathe and think. Don’t be hurry, all roads lead to Rome.

Iget reflection|01

Posted on 2020-02-10

Iget (得到) - an Chinese app that contains original audio courses on different subjects, teaching by expers and professionists.

Course taking: The psychology of self-developing. (自我发展心理学)

About The Course

This course is not an real branch of psychology but an original and fresh learning subject that the giver has extracted from real life and his 12 years experience of being a therapist. The course giver believes that the users of Iget are all ambitious and eager for advancement, but he also found that the majority are experiencing the hardship of breaking through their comfort zone and making actural changes. Therefore, he created this course about changing. This is also why we chose it to start with. As it is reality-based, a lot of connection will be made.

About This Talk ( 02 | The Essence of Changes：How to Create New Experiences)

This talk has two parts:

  1. Psychology system that hinders us from making changes

    The lecturer metioned the Jonathon・Haidt’s theory of describing our emotional and rational selfs as an selfish elephant and a clam elephant rider. The elephant is powerful, emotional, and most importantly, it’s experience-driven. This theory help me to understands that although we can imagine the benefits changes can bring to us - for instance, becoming more efficent if we stop procrastinating -, we still involuntarily choose to watch youtube as we have often experienced the joy it gives but not from being efficient. The elephant always pick the “benefits of experience” instead of the “benefits of expecting.”

  1. How to take the first step

    Using an example, the course giver suggests that, the essence of changing is the process of creating new experience and replacing the old ones.

    If you only have the expectation in your imagination but not new experiences that are gained from new behaviors, changes are barely possible to happen.

About Me

Well I would say I feel particularly connected to the content of this talk as I linked it to my experience of loosing weight. It is said that “weight losing is a life-long job of girls”, and I’m certainly one of them. I’ve been trying to get rid of my fat for several times but they all ended up with failure. On one side, I can clearly imagine the benefits of being in a good shape and I know that is exactly what I pursue. However, on the other side, I also can’t reject the feeling of craving food and eat what I like while I know I shouldn’t. Evertime when I’m facing the “junk foods”, there’s a battle between the elephant and the rider stating in my mind. They both win sometimes, but I still feel unsatisfied or regret afterward. This is a problem that I’ve been facing for a long time, and I hope the course can give me the answer.
