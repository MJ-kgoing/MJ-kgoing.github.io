CIS Summer Research Camp

Posted on 2020-08-08

What is it?

It is a summer program that allows students to work with professors from people’s dreaming colleges and research on subjects they are passionated about. The topic I chose was Personality Psychology, led by professor Zayas from Cornell University. 20 students were divided into the moring and evening sessions however we will gather together for our final project. The course begins with the knowledging learning section then progresses into the researching part and there is a chance for the final papers to be published.

Why do I attend it?

There are mainly two reasons:

  1. Obtain knowledge and research skills

    Personality is always fascinating me who is a big fan of horoscope and trait tests (and I always think they are fairly accurate until I learn about the Baren Effect.) Therefore I would love to see what really is “personality”, how we develop it and what are some studies about it. However, what in my perspective is more valuable are the research skills, including choosing topics, reading papers, creating questionair, etc. I believe they will be the core abilities required in my future studying.

  2. Learn from excellent professors and peers

    I wish I can learn from the experience of those who are older than me and expand my social circle. In addition, I wish to form a good relationship with the professor who studies the subject that I might research on in the future.

What have I obtained from it so far?

  1. Knowledge

    During the past 4 weeks we have discussed numerous topics including the defiition of personality, research methods, traits, biology and much more.The content is truly abundant that every time I review the lecture slides I learn something new. One thing that really interests me are the attachment styles as it is a topic I’ve been paying attention to. Therefore I’m looking foward to next week’s classes which will focus on it.

  2. Friendship

    Although it only has been 4 weeks but I believe a very friendly and comfortable relationship is formed between every one including the professor and the TA. Most of the students are slightly older than me therefore they often provide me with deliberate answers to my questions, beside everyone is nice and funny to talk to. It is genuienly a pleasure to meet them

What will I be doing?

This week we decided on one of the three topic and are seperated into three different groups based on the topic. We also began to read some papers on that subject which is actually challenging as some of them have more than 40 pages. Next week we will be reviewing the study and collecting data.
