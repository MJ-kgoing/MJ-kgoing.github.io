Psychology Club Brainstorm

Posted on 2020-10-31

Starting from year 11, more and more students led extra curricular activities including services and clubs pop out. For me, I already joined the school magazine contributing as a chinese writer, an economy club founded by my friends and a service that teach refugee kids to dance. In addition, my friends and I are also planing to start an art club that connect with both the school community and the society. However, I still found no perfect psychology related organization for me to attend. Therefore the idea of starting my own psychology club begins hovering in my mind.

When that idea appears, my immediately contacts my friend who I know share the same interest with me. Not surprisingly she has the same thought. We both think that, now is the perfect time to start a psychology club as it is what missing in the school. However, we are also puzzled by the format of the club should be in. Should it be academic purpose in terms of helping IB psychology students with their school work or teaching amateur psychological concepts? Or, should it be survey and research base that surves for the school education and the well-being of the students? These are the question we are considering lately. Therefore, we decides to each do some research on the common activities a school psychology club held and if necessary we will contact the Psychology subject teachers in our school in the effort of gaining support and future cooperation.
